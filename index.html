Aneja Research Foundation (ARF)

A student-led civic systems research lab focused on analyzing real-world urban problems and designing practical solutions.

ARF studies how real systems, traffic, infrastructure, and public behavior, function and fail. Through structured observation, data collection, and systems thinking, ARF produces actionable insights and solutions.

Approach

Method

ARF uses a disciplined five-step process to move from observation to practical solutions.

Step 1

Field Observation

Direct site visits establish how the system behaves under real conditions.

Step 2

Data Collection

Structured notes, counts, timings, and basic environmental evidence are gathered.

Step 3

Systems Analysis

Patterns, constraints, and recurring failure points are organized into a systems view.

Step 4

Root Cause Identification

Symptoms are separated from structural causes to identify where intervention matters.

Step 5

Solution Design

Recommendations are framed to be practical, implementable, and grounded in observed reality.
